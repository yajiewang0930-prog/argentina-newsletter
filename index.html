  • BCRA Charter Reform: Single Mandate, No Treasury Financing, Criminal Penalties

    medium

    Milei convened legislators in early July to advance a reform of the BCRA's Organic Charter — the most ambitious central bank overhaul since the 1990s Convertibility era. The bill would strip the BCRA of its multiple mandates, leaving only the preservation of currency value, explicitly prohibit monetary financing of the Treasury, and introduce criminal penalties for officials who violate the prohibition. Supporters argue the reform would lock in disinflation gains and prevent future governments from resorting to the印刷机. Critics — including former Central Bank presidents Vanoli and Hecker, and economist Bárbara Kalos — warned that eliminating the BCRA's capacity to intervene on interest rates, exchange rates, and crisis liquidity would leave Argentina defenseless against external shocks. The reform's fate in Congress remains uncertain, though the government views it as a cornerstone institutional commitment to markets ahead of 2027.

  • ARCA Activates Real-Time Monitoring of All Fintech and Bank Transfers

    high

    Argentina's tax and customs agency ARCA launched an automated real-time surveillance system in July that monitors all bank and fintech transfers, flagging accounts that exceed monthly thresholds: $50 million in transfers for individuals, $30 million for legal entities, $10 million in cash withdrawals, and $100 million in time deposits or investments. The system uses algorithmic pattern detection to identify "smurfing" — splitting large transactions into multiple small transfers to avoid detection — and can automatically restrict accounts until the holder provides supporting documentation. The thresholds are updated monthly based on inflation. Fintech platforms, which process a growing share of Argentina's payment flows through CVU accounts, are now subject to the same monitoring intensity as traditional banks. The measure responds to IMF and FATF recommendations to close regulatory gaps in Argentina's digital payments infrastructure, but fintech associations have expressed concern about the compliance burden on smaller firms without dedicated legal teams.
