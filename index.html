However, weak domestic consumption and a record surge in household credit delinquencies are tempering optimism heading into H2 2026.

🏦 Financial & Credit

Argentina's credit market is flashing two contradictory signals. Aggregate private-sector credit inched up 0.6% in real terms in April, reversing a two-month contraction, yet credit penetration remains anemic at just 9.3% of GDP — far below the 40–60% Latin American average. Meanwhile, household non-performing loan ratios have surged to their highest levels in nearly two decades, driven by a toxic combination of real wage erosion, rapid fintech credit expansion, and deregulatory policies under DNU 70/2023.

  • Household NPL Ratio Hits 11.5% — 5.3 Million Argentines in Default

    high

    Argentina's household non-performing loan ratio reached 11.5% in March 2026, more than tripling from 3.3% in March 2025 and hitting its highest level in nearly two decades, according to BCRA data. Personal loan delinquencies stand at 14.2%, credit card defaults at 11.7%, and auto loan delinquencies at 6.9%. A staggering 5.3 million Argentines — 26.7% of all borrowers — now have at least one loan in irregular status. The surge is attributed to a 12% decline in real disposable income versus pre-pandemic levels, with many households having used credit to cover basic living expenses. The average overdue debt is approximately ARS 5.9 million (~$4,100), predominantly from supermarket, pharmacy, and essential services consumption. Corporate NPLs remain comparatively contained at 3.1%.

💳 Fintech

Argentina's fintech sector faces a defining moment. The IMF's staff report explicitly called out non-bank credit — now 15% of total banking credit — as a systemic risk, with delinquency rates exceeding 30% industry-wide and reaching 50% at some lenders. Naranja X dominates with 37.7% market share, followed by Mercado Pago at 14.8%. Meanwhile, Ualá leads the digital wallet rate war at 26% TNA, and Congress is advancing legislation that would require wallets to block payments to illegal gambling sites.

  • IMF Flags Fintech Systemic Risk, Urges Stricter Virtual Wallet Supervision

    high

    The IMF's May 2026 staff report devoted unprecedented attention to Argentina's non-bank credit sector, explicitly urging the government to strengthen supervision of fintech lenders and virtual wallets. Non-bank credit now accounts for 15% of total banking credit, with overall delinquency rates exceeding 30% and reaching as high as 50% at some institutions. The share of "irrecoverable" loans in the non-bank segment surged from 2.6% in March 2025 to 10.8% in March 2026. The IMF linked the deterioration to the deregulatory DNU 70/2023, which removed interest rate caps, eliminated the requirement to disclose the total financial cost (CFT) to borrowers, and weakened consumer protections. The report recommends implementing consolidated supervision across banks and non-banks, imposing prudential limits on fintech lending, and mandating credit bureau reporting for all digital lenders.
