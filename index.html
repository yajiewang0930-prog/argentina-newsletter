Key Developments

The BCRA bought over USD 7 billion in the first four months, but inflation remains stubbornly high, and household financial stress is deepening, with morosidad (delinquency) surpassing pandemic levels.

Financial Markets

Key Developments

The Argentine peso remains stable across official and parallel markets, with the blue dollar steady near ARS 1,200. However, a significant ARS 6.9 trillion debt maturity wall in May is testing the Treasury’s rollover strategy, while rising delinquency in Greater Buenos Aires—quadrupling in the past year—signals growing consumer stress despite official claims the peak has passed.

International Impact

Key Developments

Argentina’s external debt hit a record $483 billion, with short-term maturities quadrupling, as the government executed a $819 million SDR purchase from the U.S. Treasury to service IMF interest payments. While Wall Street remains cautiously optimistic about Milei’s program, a key negotiator warns the debt is now less repayable, and a top official resigned after plans to return to capital markets were shelved.
